## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Retesting Retired Root Causes

**What goes wrong:** The plan spends time retesting orchestration starvation, baud domains, UART RX, board health, chip health, static init framing, reset/basic bring-up, or safe-prerequisite gates. [VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-CONTEXT.md`]

**Why it happens:** Phase 28.1 produced extensive blocker-isolation evidence, but Phase 28.1.1 starts in the middle of a hardware investigation. [VERIFIED: `.planning/PROJECT.md`; VERIFIED: `.planning/STATE.md`]

**How to avoid:** Treat `firmware-nonce-production` as the baseline and plan only post-fix Rust rerun, upstream scratch field diff, and one-divergence fixes. [VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-CONTEXT.md`]

**Warning signs:** A task proposes new bridge modes or timeout behavior before proving a field-level init/job divergence. [VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-CONTEXT.md`]

### Pitfall 2: Promoting Without Live Submit Outcome

**What goes wrong:** STR-09 or CFG-07 is marked verified from wire-looking-good evidence, register-read proof, or work dispatch counts. [VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-CONTEXT.md`; VERIFIED: `.planning/ROADMAP.md`]

**Why it happens:** The existing logs contain healthy markers such as work dispatch and register-read parsing, but run-05 still had zero correlated nonce and zero accepted/rejected shares. [VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-run-wire-parity-redacted.md`]

**How to avoid:** Require `asic_production_status=result_correlated` plus accepted/rejected `share_submission_status` in committed redacted evidence before Phase 30 promotion. [VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-CONTEXT.md`; VERIFIED: `.planning/ROADMAP.md`]

**Warning signs:** Evidence mentions only `work_dispatched`, `register_read_parsed`, or `power_delta` without a submit response category. [VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-run-wire-parity-redacted.md`]

### Pitfall 3: Leaking Sensitive Runtime Values

**What goes wrong:** Raw pool endpoints, ports, users, workers, owner addresses, passwords, tokens, device URLs, IPs, MACs, SSIDs, NVS secret values, share payloads, job bytes, or BM1366 frames enter committed docs. [VERIFIED: `AGENTS.md`; VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-CONTEXT.md`; VERIFIED: user prompt]

**Why it happens:** The decisive diagnostics are raw wire and live pool captures, while committed artifacts must be field-level summaries only. [VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-CONTEXT.md`]

[VERIFIED: `AGENTS.md`; VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-run-wire-parity-redacted.md`]

**Warning signs:** A proposed committed artifact includes raw JSON-RPC params, raw UART hex streams, endpoint values, worker strings, or unredacted board network identifiers. [VERIFIED: `AGENTS.md`; VERIFIED: `.planning/phases/28.1.1-bm1366-nonce-production-wire-parity/28.1.1-CONTEXT.md`]
